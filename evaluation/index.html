EVALUATION PHILOSOPHY

We are not optimizing for:

  • • Speed
  • • Volume
  • • Ego
  • • Titles

We are optimizing for:

  • • Responsibility
  • • Verification discipline
  • • Systems thinking
  • • Clarity under uncertainty
  • • Real-world risk awareness

SCORING OVERVIEW

Each candidate is evaluated across 7 dimensions.

  • • Score each dimension 0–5
  • • Add reviewer notes
  • • Flag risks immediately
  • • Outcome is Accept / Advance / Hold / Decline

Maximum Score: 35
Passing Range: Contextual, scores matter less than red flags

DIMENSION 1,
RESPONSIBILITY MINDSET (0–5)

Question Signal Sources:

  • • “Responsibility” definition
  • • Harm-related questions
  • • Shipping under uncertainty responses

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Avoids responsibility / deflects blame
  • • 1 Responsibility only within narrow role
  • • 2 Acknowledges responsibility but prioritizes speed
  • • 3 Balances delivery with safety
  • • 4 Proactively protects users and systems
  • • 5 Explicitly prioritizes harm prevention over progress

Red Flags

  • • “That’s not my problem” language
  • • Willingness to ship harmful systems

DIMENSION 2,
VERIFICATION DISCIPLINE (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • Verification methodology answers
  • • Evidence vs assumption language

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Relies on intuition or authority
  • • 1 Occasional checks
  • • 2 Standard checks without limits
  • • 3 Repeatable verification methods
  • • 4 Verification + awareness of failure modes
  • • 5 Verification + escalation rules + stop conditions

Red Flags

  • • “I just know”
  • • Overconfidence without method

DIMENSION 3,
SYSTEMS THINKING (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • Descriptions of real-world impact
  • • Awareness of downstream effects

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Isolated task thinking
  • • 1 Team-level only
  • • 2 Product-level awareness
  • • 3 System-level interactions
  • • 4 Cross-domain consequences
  • • 5 End-to-end system risk awareness

Red Flags

  • • Ignores downstream harm
  • • Treats failures as isolated

DIMENSION 4,
HUMILITY & LEARNING (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • “Confident and wrong” response
  • • Willingness to say “I don’t know”

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Never wrong
  • • 1 Deflects errors
  • • 2 Acknowledges errors reluctantly
  • • 3 Learns after mistakes
  • • 4 Actively seeks correction
  • • 5 Builds systems to catch their own errors

Red Flags

  • • Defensive tone
  • • Refusal to revise beliefs

DIMENSION 5,
RISK & HARM AWARENESS (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • Harm scenarios
  • • Safety boundaries

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Ignores harm
  • • 1 Sees harm as unlikely
  • • 2 Acknowledges harm abstractly
  • • 3 Identifies realistic risks
  • • 4 Designs to reduce harm
  • • 5 Designs to prevent and detect harm early

Red Flags

  • • “Edge cases don’t matter”
  • • Minimizes real-world consequences

DIMENSION 6,
COMMUNICATION CLARITY (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • Written responses
  • • Structure and precision

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Confusing / incoherent
  • • 1 Rambling
  • • 2 Understandable but unfocused
  • • 3 Clear and direct
  • • 4 Clear with examples
  • • 5 Clear, precise, and scoped

Red Flags

  • • Buzzwords without meaning
  • • Avoids direct answers

DIMENSION 7,
CULTURAL ALIGNMENT (0–5)

Signal Sources:

  • • Speed vs verification choice
  • • Values alignment

Scoring Guide

  • • 0 Strongly misaligned
  • • 1 Weak alignment
  • • 2 Neutral
  • • 3 Generally aligned
  • • 4 Strong alignment
  • • 5 Naturally embodies ALETHRA™ values

Red Flags

  • • “Move fast and fix later” preference
  • • Dismisses verification culture

OUTCOME GUIDANCE
(NOT AUTOMATIC)

Advance Immediately

  • • No red flags
  • • Strong scores in Responsibility, Verification, and Risk

Advance with Caution

  • • Strong skills, minor gaps
  • • Pair with senior oversight

Hold

  • • Unclear alignment
  • • Revisit after clarification

Decline

  • • Any major red flag
  • • Misalignment of responsibility or harm
