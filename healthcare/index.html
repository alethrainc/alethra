PROBLEM

Healthcare systems operate under intense pressure, yet critical processes still rely on unverified information. Patient records, medication labels, specimen samples, wristbands, blood products, diet restrictions, and device settings all carry the risk of being incorrect or mismatched.

Senior care environments face the same vulnerabilities - misidentification, dosing errors, and environmental hazards.

One unverified input can rapidly escalate into patient harm, legal exposure, and catastrophic loss of trust.

BREAKDOWN

The breakdown occurs because healthcare workflows assume accuracy without proving it.

A medication barcode may match the system, but the vial may be mislabeled. A patient wristband may scan correctly, but the underlying record may be wrong. A surgical tray may pass inspection, but critical items may be missing.

Senior care routines rely on visual checks and human memory - highly inconsistent in real-world environments.

The system moves fast, yet the underlying verification is slow, fragmented, or nonexistent.

Errors hide until they cause damage.
